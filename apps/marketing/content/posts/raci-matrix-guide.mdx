## What is a RACI Matrix?

A RACI matrix (also called a Responsibility Assignment Matrix) maps every activity in a process to the people involved, using four assignment types:

- **R — Responsible**: Who does the work
- **A — Accountable**: Who makes the final decision (only one per activity)
- **C — Consulted**: Who provides input before the decision
- **I — Informed**: Who is notified after the decision

## Why Every Process Needs a RACI

Without clear role assignments, processes suffer from:
- **Confusion** — "I thought you were handling that"
- **Bottlenecks** — Everyone waits for the same person
- **Gaps** — Nobody owns the handoff between departments
- **Duplicated work** — Two teams doing the same thing

A RACI matrix eliminates ambiguity by making every assignment explicit.

## The 3-Step RACI Method

### Step 1: List Activities (rows)
Extract the key activities from your process. Use the same activities from your BPMN diagram or process map.

### Step 2: List Roles (columns)
Identify every role involved. Use role titles, not individual names — roles survive personnel changes.

### Step 3: Assign R, A, C, I
For each cell, ask: Is this role Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, or Informed for this activity?

**Key rule**: Every row must have exactly one **A** (Accountable). If an activity has zero or two A's, the assignment is broken.

## Common RACI Mistakes

1. **Too many C's and I's** — If everyone is consulted on everything, nobody moves fast
2. **Missing the A** — Every activity needs exactly one accountable person
3. **Using names instead of roles** — Roles are more durable
4. **Making it too granular** — RACI works best at the activity level, not the task level
