## What is a SIPOC Diagram?

SIPOC stands for **Suppliers, Inputs, Process, Outputs, and Customers**. It's a high-level process mapping tool used by BPM consultants, Six Sigma practitioners, and process analysts to understand the scope and boundaries of a process before diving into detailed mapping.

A SIPOC diagram answers five fundamental questions:

1. **Suppliers** — Who provides the inputs to this process?
2. **Inputs** — What materials, information, or resources enter the process?
3. **Process** — What are the 5-8 high-level steps?
4. **Outputs** — What does this process produce?
5. **Customers** — Who receives the outputs?

## When to Use a SIPOC

- **Before a process elicitation session** — to scope the conversation
- **At the beginning of a Six Sigma project** — to define process boundaries
- **When onboarding new team members** — to give them a high-level overview
- **During process audits** — to verify that all stakeholders are identified

## How to Create a SIPOC in 5 Steps

1. **Start with the Process** — Define 5-8 high-level steps using verb + noun format ("Receive request", "Review budget", "Approve order")
2. **Identify Outputs** — What does each step produce?
3. **Identify Customers** — Who receives each output?
4. **Identify Inputs** — What does the process need to start?
5. **Identify Suppliers** — Who provides each input?

## Common Mistakes

- Making the process steps too detailed (keep it to 5-8 high-level steps)
- Confusing internal handoffs with suppliers/customers
- Forgetting to include system inputs (data, templates, policies)
